Academic Profile

Romina Norouzi is a bioinformatics researcher working at the interface of computational drug design, molecular biology, and biomedical artificial intelligence. Her research integrates molecular representation learning, generative modeling, and bioinformatics analysis to support data-driven therapeutic discovery.


Her work is motivated by the question of how artificial intelligence models can learn from biomedical and molecular data to generate, evaluate, and prioritize meaningful candidate molecules. She is also interested in expanding her future research toward multi-modal biomedical frameworks that connect molecular structures, omics data, high-content imaging, Cell Painting profiles, and organoid-based disease models for drug design.


Her broader background includes molecular genetics, cell and molecular biology, multiomics data analysis, genome and transcriptome assembly, and experimental molecular biology techniques.
