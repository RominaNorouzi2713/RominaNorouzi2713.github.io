About

I am Romina Norouzi, a bioinformatics researcher with a Ph.D. in Bioinformatics. I completed my doctoral studies from 2021 to 2026 in the Department of Bioinformatics at the Institute of Biochemistry and Biophysics, University of Tehran, within the Laboratory of Bioinformatics and Drug Design. My research centers on artificial intelligence for biomedical data, computational drug design, molecular biology, and data-driven therapeutic discovery.


My work is motivated by the use of AI models to learn from biomedical and molecular data, generate candidate structures, evaluate molecular properties, and prioritize biologically meaningful therapeutic hypotheses. My broader background includes molecular genetics, cell and molecular biology, multiomics data analysis, de novo genome and transcriptome assembly, and experimental molecular biology techniques.
