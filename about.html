About

I am Romina Norouzi, Ph.D. in Bioinformatics, awarded after my doctoral studies from 2021 to 2026 at the Department of Bioinformatics, Institute of Biochemistry and Biophysics, University of Tehran, in the Laboratory of Bioinformatics and Drug Design. My research focuses on de novo molecule design for specific targets using deep generative models, with broader interests in computational drug design, cheminformatics, molecular generation, molecular biology, and biomedical data analysis.


I am especially interested in how AI models can generate pharmacologically meaningful molecular fragments and assemble them into candidate molecules for drug discovery. My background also includes molecular genetics, cell and molecular biology, multiomics data analysis, de novo genome and transcriptome assembly, and experimental molecular biology techniques.
