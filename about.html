Academic Profile

As a bioinformatics researcher working at the interface of computational drug design, molecular biology, and biomedical artificial intelligence, my research integrates molecular representation learning, generative modeling, and bioinformatics analysis to support data-driven therapeutic discovery.


My work is motivated by the question of how artificial intelligence models can learn from biomedical and molecular data to generate, evaluate, and prioritize meaningful candidate molecules. I am also interested in expanding my future research toward multi-modal biomedical frameworks that connect molecular structures, omics data, high-content imaging, Cell Painting profiles, and organoid-based disease models for drug design.


My broader background includes molecular genetics, cell and molecular biology, multiomics data analysis, genome and transcriptome assembly, and experimental molecular biology techniques.
